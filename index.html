What this does to Boulevard East.

It darkens the corridor

A seven story wall means less sky, less late day light, and a heavier street edge. Kids should not have to play in the shadow of a building that never should have been approved at this scale.

It adds street pressure

More garage trips, deliveries, ride share stops, moving activity, and curb use mean more conflict on streets that already feel squeezed.

It weakens the view experience

Boulevard East is valued because it feels open to the river and skyline. Wall to wall development chips away at that civic experience.

It strains parking

The zoning table lists 128 parking stalls. The approved proposal has 80. Missing parking does not disappear. It moves onto the block.

It makes access harder

When a building is pushed almost to the edges of the site, practical access for service, maintenance, and emergency response becomes harder.

It sets a damaging precedent

Once one medium density site is treated like high density, every nearby property becomes vulnerable to the next oversized application.

This is the message to neighbors.

This is not just about one development site. It is about whether Boulevard East remains the Jewel of the Hudson or becomes a canyon of oversized buildings.
